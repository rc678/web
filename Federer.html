Roger Federer

He currently has 17 grand slams which is the most grand slams anyone has won. Federer holds several records of the Open Era. Firstly, for holding the world No. 1 position for 302 weeks which is including 237 consecutive weeks. Furthermore, he also has a record for reaching each Grand Slam final at least five times which is an all-time record. He also reached the Wimbledon final ten times.

He is among the seven men and also among the four in Open Era to capture a career Grand Slam. Federer shares an Open Era record for most titles at Wimbledon with Pete Sampras and at the US Open with Jimmy Connors and Sampras. Federer has reached 27 men's singles Grand Slam finals.This includes reaching 10 finals in a row from the 2005 Wimbledon Championships to the 2007 US Open and both those numbers are record breaking. He also appeared in 18 of 19 finals from the 2005 Wimbledon through to the 2010 Australian Open.He reached the semifinals at 23 consecutive Grand Slam tournaments,from the 2004 Wimbledon Championships through the 2010 Australian Open. At the 2016 Australian Open, he played in a record 65th consecutive Grand Slam tournament,reached a record 47th Grand Slam quarterfinal and a record 39th Grand Slam semifinal.

Earlier at the 2015 US Open, he reached a record 27th Grand Slam final as he did not reach the final in the 2016 Australian Open. Federer has won the most matches in Grand Slams by winning 302 matches and is the first to have a record of more than 65 wins at each Grand Slam tournament. He also won the Olympic gold medal in doubles with his partner Stan Wawrinka at the 2008 Summer Olympic Games and the Olympic silver medal in singles atthe 2012 Summer Olympic Games. Federer also won a record six ATP World Tour Finals, playing in the finals at all nine ATP Masters 1000 tournaments which is a record also shared by Djokovic and Nadal. He also has won the most prize money than any player in history.
