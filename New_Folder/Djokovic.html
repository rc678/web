Novak Djokovic


Djokovic is currently ranked the world's number one player and he currently has 11 grand slams. He is ranked the 4th player to win the most number of slams and is tied with Bjorn Borg who also has won 11 slams. He has held the No. 1 spot in the ATP rankings for a total of 186 weeks. In 2011 he has won 3 of the 4 grand slams in a calendar year so he became the sixth male player to win three Grand Slams in a calendar year, which he did so again in 2015. Djokovic has won an all-time record six Australian Open titles. He is also the first and only person to have an open era record of three consecutive titles from 2011–2013.

In addition to the grand slams, he has won the ATP World Tour Finals five times which is an Open Era record. He also was on the Serbian team for the 2010 davis cup and won. He also won the Bronze medal in men's singles
at the 2008 Summer Olympics. He has won 26 Masters 1000 series title which broke the single-season record with six titles in 2015 which put him second on the list of Masters 1000 winners since its inception in 1990. Djokovic is
the first Serbian player to be ranked No. 1 by the ATP and he is the first male player representing Serbia to win a Grand Slam singles title.
