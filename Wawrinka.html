Stan Wawrinka

He currently has 2 grand slams and is ranked world's number 3 player. He has won the 2014 Australian Open and the 2015 French Open. He also won an Association of Tennis Professionals (ATP) Masters 1000 tournament at the Monte-Carlo Masters in 2014, and was a finalist at the Rome Masters in 2008 and the Madrid Masters in 2013. Furthermore, he won a gold medal for Switzerland in the men's doubles event at the 2008 Summer Olympics, partnering with Roger Federer, and was part of the Swiss team that won the Davis Cup in 2014. He played in the longest doubles match in history at the Davis Cup tie against the Czech Republic in 2013, partnering with Marco Chiudinelli. He is one of the few active players on tour to have reached the quarterfinal stage of all four Grand Slams.

He is also one of only three players to have Grand Slam wins against each of the Big Four players such as Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, Novak Djokovic and Andy Murray and one of only two players to have three wins on tour level against each of the Big Four players. He has two wins against reigning world number one players in his career, both happened to come in the final. One was over Nadal in Australian Open 2014 and the second was over Djokovic in French Open 2015. He is the second player to defeat both Nadal and Djokovic in a grand slam final as the first player was Federer.
